Subflow Health Overview
Subflow Health is a healthcare technology company focused on helping organizations
deliver evidence-based care beyond the traditional visit. At its core, Subflow Health is a care
orchestration, automation, and patient engagement platform built to extend care outside the
walls of the clinic, improve adherence, reduce manual administrative burden, and give care
teams better visibility into what is happening between encounters.
Subflow is not just a texting tool, and it is not an EHR. It is the layer that sits around and
between healthcare workflows to help care teams design, automate, deliver, and measure
care programs over time. The platform is designed for longitudinal care, meaning it supports
the entire journey of care before, during, after, and between visits rather than focusing only
on isolated point-in-time interactions.
The company’s broader vision is to become the infrastructure that helps healthcare
organizations orchestrate care programs across patients, care teams, providers, payers, and
partners. The goal is to make it easier for healthcare organizations to deliver timely
guidance, automate repetitive tasks, improve patient follow-through, and collect actionable
data that helps drive better decisions and outcomes.
A core part of Subflow Health’s identity is that it enables frictionless patient access. The
company strongly believes that many patient engagement tools fail because they depend too
heavily on patient portals, apps, passwords, and logins. Subflow is built around the idea that
care should meet patients where they already are, especially through channels like text
messaging, email, and simple web experiences that reduce friction and increase
participation.
What Subflow Health Is
Subflow Health is best understood as a care orchestration and engagement platform for
healthcare.
It helps healthcare organizations:
● automate workflows tied to patient care
● deliver education, reminders, surveys, and follow-up content
● monitor patient engagement and adherence
● guide patients to next steps in their care journey
● reduce manual staff workload
● create structured digital care programs
● capture data throughout the patient journey
● surface insights that help teams intervene earlier
The platform supports both operational efficiency and clinical engagement. That means it is
useful both for reducing staff burden and for improving patient participation in care plans,
recovery plans, chronic condition programs, referrals, and other longitudinal workflows.
Subflow can support a wide range of workflows, including onboarding, intake, pre-op
preparation, post-op recovery, referral coordination, ongoing chronic care management,
preventive care outreach, surveys, education delivery, and care navigation.
What Subflow Health Is Not
It is important for a support team to understand what Subflow is not, because that shapes
both customer expectations and product messaging.
Subflow is not:
● an EHR or EMR
● a system of record for full clinical documentation
● a basic bulk SMS marketing platform
● a patient portal that depends on app downloads and complex logins
● a replacement for all clinical systems
● just a survey tool or just a content library
Instead, it complements the systems providers already use. It acts as a workflow and
engagement layer that can integrate with core systems and make care delivery outside of
visits more scalable and measurable.
The Core Problem Subflow Solves
Healthcare organizations often deliver care well during a visit, but struggle to manage what
happens before the visit, after the visit, or between visits. A large amount of important care
depends on patient follow-through outside of the clinic:
● reading education materials
● preparing correctly for procedures
● completing rehab exercises
● reporting symptoms
● filling out forms
● responding to reminders
● attending referrals
● showing up to appointments
● understanding next steps
● adhering to care plans over time
These activities are often managed manually by staff through phone calls, paper handouts,
portal messages, spreadsheets, or fragmented systems. This creates several common
problems:
● staff spend too much time on repetitive manual tasks
● patients forget or ignore instructions
● engagement rates are inconsistent
● organizations cannot easily measure what patients actually did
● care teams lack visibility into risk and drop-off points
● patients fall through the cracks between visits
● follow-up workflows are hard to scale
Subflow solves this by helping organizations create automated, trackable, patient-friendly
workflows that drive action and make follow-through easier.

That means the platform helps organizations take real care pathways, education protocols,

The value proposition typically includes:
● improve adherence
● reduce manual burden on staff

How the Platform Thinks About Care Delivery
Subflow is built around the idea that care can be designed, automated, delivered, monitored,
and improved in a structured way.
A helpful internal model is that Subflow supports five major layers:
1. Design Layer
This is how care is structured.
Organizations can define digital care programs, often referred to as care plans or flows,
made up of phases, steps, and tasks. These can represent real patient journeys such as:
● new patient onboarding
● pre-op preparation
● post-op recovery
● pulmonary rehab
● chronic care follow-up
● referral coordination
● educational pathways
● population health check-ins
The design layer gives teams a way to organize what should happen and when it should
happen.
2. Execution Layer
This is how care is automated and delivered.
Using automation logic, Subflow can trigger steps based on events such as:
● patient creation
● a scheduled date
● a surgery date
● form submission
● a missed task
● a discharge event
● referral creation
● survey response
● external system updates
The system then takes action such as:
● sending a text or email
● assigning a task
● progressing a patient to the next step
● notifying staff
● delivering education
● sending a survey
● triggering another automation
3. Engagement Layer
This is how patients and care teams participate.
Subflow helps organizations engage patients through low-friction communication methods
like:
● SMS/text
● email
● secure web-based experiences
● chat
● digital forms
● educational content
● live or scheduled interactions, depending on customer implementation
The goal is to reduce barriers so patients actually participate.
4. Intelligence Layer
This is how activity becomes insight.
Subflow captures engagement and workflow data across the patient journey. That can
include:
● messages sent
● messages received
● link clicks
● content views
● form completions
● survey responses
● task completions
● drop-off points
● adherence indicators
● patient-reported outcomes
This can be used to identify who is engaging, who is not, where people are falling behind,
and what signals suggest a need for intervention.
5. Action Layer
This is how insight turns into action.
When the platform detects an issue or reaches a rule threshold, it can help organizations
respond by:
● alerting a care team member
● assigning a follow-up task
● escalating a patient workflow
● triggering the next message or resource
● surfacing a patient as higher risk
● guiding the patient toward the next appropriate step of care
Key Platform Features
Below is a more detailed breakdown of important features and functional areas in Subflow.
Patient Communication
Subflow supports patient communication across multiple channels, with SMS as a major
strength because of its high open rates and low friction. This enables care teams to send:
● reminders
● educational messages
● updates
● follow-up prompts
● surveys
● resource links
● calls to action
Communication can be one-way or two-way depending on the workflow and implementation.
The emphasis is on making communication timely, relevant, and simple for the patient to
engage with.
Automation Workflows
Automation is one of the most important parts of Subflow.
Customers can build workflows that trigger actions based on specific dates, events, or
patient behavior. This allows organizations to reduce manual coordination and create more
reliable follow-up.
Examples of automation actions include:
● sending pre-op instructions seven days before surgery
● reminding a patient to complete intake forms
● following up after discharge
● checking whether a patient completed a survey
● escalating non-response to staff
● assigning an internal task after a patient reports a concerning symptom
● moving a patient to the next care phase based on completion of a milestone
This is critical for scale because it allows a smaller team to manage a much larger patient
population with more consistency.
Care Plans / Structured Programs
Subflow supports structured care journeys. These are often built as phases or step-based
programs that reflect how care should happen over time.
A care plan may include:
● educational milestones
● tasks for the patient
● tasks for staff
● forms and surveys
● automated messages
● timelines tied to clinical events
● conditional branching based on patient responses
This allows care delivery to become more standardized, while still supporting
personalization.
Forms
Forms are used to collect information from patients or other users. These can support:
● onboarding
● intake
● contact information collection
● consent workflows
● symptom collection
● surveys
● screening questions
● updates to patient records
Forms are important because they give customers a flexible way to gather needed
information and trigger automations based on responses.
Surveys and Patient-Reported Outcomes
Subflow can be used to collect patient feedback and outcomes data.
This includes:
● NPS surveys
● satisfaction surveys
● symptom check-ins
● patient-reported outcome measures
● adherence check-ins
● recovery progress surveys
This is valuable both for direct care and for measuring program effectiveness.
Contact and Patient Record Management
Subflow includes a patient/contact layer that stores core information used to power
communications and workflows. Depending on implementation, this may include:
● name
● phone number
● email
● date-based milestones
● procedure details
● care plan enrollment
● tags or segmentation data
● form and survey history
● engagement history
The platform is not intended to replace the EHR as the legal system of record, but it does
maintain enough structured patient data to support workflows and engagement.
Segmentation
Customers can create segments or groups based on attributes or behavior.
This can help support:
● outreach to certain patient populations
● filtering by diagnosis or procedure type
● identifying non-responders
● targeting education to the right groups
● tracking populations with shared program criteria
Segmentation improves relevance and allows more tailored engagement.
Content Delivery
Content is a major part of Subflow’s value.
Organizations can host and deliver content such as:
● educational articles
● post-op instructions
● exercise plans
● wellness resources
● clinical guidance
● videos
● course-like experiences
● links to external resources
This content can be embedded into workflows and sent at the right time in the patient
journey. A big part of Subflow’s approach is helping organizations deliver the right content at
the right moment rather than dumping everything into a portal and expecting patients to go
find it.
Messaging and Conversations
Subflow supports direct communication workflows and can enable staff-to-patient
engagement in a more centralized and trackable way than personal texting or disconnected
systems.
This can help organizations:
● maintain brand consistency
● protect privacy
● reduce dependence on personal cell phones
● centralize communication history
● make outreach more measurable
Tasks and Internal Follow-Up
Subflow can create and manage tasks tied to workflows. These tasks may be for internal
staff or related to patient progress.
Examples:
● follow up with a patient who has not completed a form
● review a survey response
● call a patient flagged as at-risk
● confirm referral completion
● assign next-step outreach to a coordinator
This helps bridge automation and human intervention.
Analytics and Reporting
Subflow captures engagement and workflow data that can be used to report on operational
and clinical program performance.
Metrics may include:
● message delivery and response rates
● survey completion
● content engagement
● workflow completion
● adherence by program
● task completion
● patient participation
● NPS and satisfaction
● risk flags
● referral completion
● engagement over time
This reporting helps customers understand what is working, where friction exists, and how
their programs are performing.
AI and Insights
Subflow has a growing emphasis on intelligence and AI-driven insights.
The broader vision is to use workflow activity, engagement patterns, and patient responses
to help organizations:
● identify risk earlier
● spot drop-off trends
● understand who needs intervention
● automate next-best actions
● improve program effectiveness over time
This may include AI-assisted segmentation, insight generation, or workflow intelligence
depending on the product maturity and implementation context.
Integration Support
Subflow is designed to work alongside external systems.
Common integration goals include:
● creating patient records from an EHR
● syncing surgery dates or appointment dates
● triggering pre-op and post-op workflows
● receiving discharge events
● pushing outcomes or engagement data back into another system
● receiving referral-related data
● syncing updates when dates or statuses change
The platform is API-first and built to support integration patterns rather than operate in a silo.
Who Subflow Health Serves
Subflow serves healthcare organizations that need a better way to automate and manage
care outside traditional encounters.
The target customer types can include:
Community Health Centers
CHCs often deal with high patient needs, limited staffing, and large manual follow-up
workloads. Subflow can help extend care outside the clinic, improve communication, support
education and navigation, and reduce staff burden for underserved populations.
Multi-Site Provider Groups and MSOs
Larger provider organizations need consistency across locations and workflows. Subflow
can help standardize communication, automate common programs, and create repeatable
patient journeys across multiple sites.
Specialty Clinics
Specialty clinics often have important pre-visit, post-visit, or between-visit follow-up needs.
Examples include orthopedic, rehab, cardiology, pulmonary, weight loss, and chronic disease
clinics.
Ambulatory Surgery Centers
ASCs are a strong fit because surgery preparation and recovery require structured,
time-sensitive communication and follow-up. Subflow can automate pre-op education,
reminders, recovery check-ins, and outcome collection.
Physical Therapy and Rehab Organizations
These groups benefit from improved adherence, exercise education delivery, remote
follow-up, progress tracking, and communication between visits.
Health and Wellness Programs
Subflow can support virtual coaching, wellness subscriptions, lifestyle programs,
obesity/weight loss engagement, and other programs where ongoing communication and
adherence matter.
Payers and Value-Based Care Organizations
Payers and risk-bearing entities care about adherence, outcomes, utilization reduction, and
longitudinal engagement. Subflow can support outreach, education, navigation, reminders,
and engagement strategies tied to better total cost of care.
Hospitals and Health Systems
Hospitals can use Subflow for discharge follow-up, referral support, care transitions,
education, and downstream care coordination.
Clinical Trials and Research Programs
Trial participation depends heavily on patient adherence, education, follow-through, and
ongoing engagement. Subflow can help automate reminders, instructions, check-ins, and
participant communication.
Employer Health and Population Health Programs
Subflow can support longitudinal engagement models that aim to improve preventive
behavior, condition management, or care participation over time.

Common Product Themes Support Should Understand
There are several themes that come up repeatedly in how Subflow is positioned and used.
Frictionless Access
A central belief in the product is that fewer barriers lead to higher participation. This is why
SMS and low-friction web access matter so much in the product story.
Automation With Human Oversight
Subflow is not about replacing care teams. It is about automating repetitive work and making
it easier for staff to intervene when human attention is actually needed.
Longitudinal Care
Subflow is designed for ongoing journeys, not just single messages or isolated
appointments.
Evidence-Based Delivery
The platform is intended to help organizations deliver real, structured care and education
based on their programs and protocols, not random outreach.
Measurable Engagement
Subflow places strong value on capturing data about what patients actually do, not just what
was sent.
Actionable Insight
The data collected should lead to intervention, prioritization, and better workflows, not just sit
in a dashboard.
